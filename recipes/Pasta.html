Ingridients

  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt
  • Crushed tomatoes (or diced tomatoes)
  • Parmesan cheese (for serving, optional)
  • Pasta (e.g., spaghetti, penne, fusilli)
  • Black pepper
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Garlic cloves
  • Dried oregano

    • Steps

    1. Bring Water to a Boil: Fill a large pot with water, add a generous pinch of salt, and bring it to a rolling boil over high heat.
    2. Cook the Pasta: Once the water is boiling, add your chosen pasta (see ingredients below). Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Cook according to package directions until al dente (firm to the bite).
    3. Prepare the Sauce Base (While Pasta Cooks): While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet or pan over medium heat.
    4. Sauté Aromatics: Add the minced garlic to the hot oil and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Be careful not to burn it.
    5. Add Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoAdd Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes (or diced tomatoes with their juice) into the skillet. Stir well.es (or diced tomatoes with their juice) into the skillet. Stir well.
    6. Season the Sauce: Add the dried oregano, red pepper flakes (if using), and a good pinch of salt and black pepper to the sauce. Stir to combine.
    7. Simmer the Sauce: Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer and let it cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly.
    8. Add Fresh Basil: Once the sauce has simmered, tear or roughly chop the fresh basil leaves and stir them into the sauce. Cook for another minute or two, just until the basil wilts.
    9. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Before draining the pasta, reserve about 1/2 cup of the starchy pasta water. Drain the cooked pasta. Add the drained pasta directly to the skillet with the sauce.
    10. Toss and Adjust: Toss the pasta with the sauce until it's well coated. If the sauce seems too thick, add a tablespoon or two of the reserved pasta water at a time until you reach your desired consistency.
